FANTA

Flavour Updated

Fanta is a fruit-flavored carbonated soft drink created by The Coca-Cola Company. It was first introduced in Germany in 1940 as a result of trade embargoes during World War II, which made it difficult to import Coca-Cola syrup into the country. The name "Fanta" is derived from the German word "Fantasie," meaning imagination or fantasy. Over the years, Fanta has expanded its flavor offerings and is now available in various countries worldwide, with flavors ranging from orange and grape to exotic fruits like passion fruit and lychee.
